 # ![[Texts/老子/老子16#^KR5c0057xtlsx016-1az3-h]]
Returning to the root {To those who will not admit morality without a deity to validate it, or spirituality of which man is not the measure, the firmness of Lao Tzu’s morality and the sweetness of his spiritual counsel must seem incomprehensible, or illegitimate, or very troubling indeed.}

   ![[Texts/老子/老子16#^KR5c0057xtlsx016-1az3]]
Be completely empty.

![[Texts/老子/老子16#^KR5c0057xtlsx016-1az4]]
Be perfectly serene.

![[Texts/老子/老子16#^KR5c0057xtlsx016-1az5]]
The ten thousand things arise together;

![[Texts/老子/老子16#^KR5c0057xtlsx016-1az6]]
in their arising is their return.

![[Texts/老子/老子16#^KR5c0057xtlsx016-1az7]]
Now they flower, and flowering

![[Texts/老子/老子16#^KR5c0057xtlsx016-1az8]]
sink homeward, returning to the root.

![[Texts/老子/老子16#^KR5c0057xtlsx016-1az9]]
The return to the root is peace.

![[Texts/老子/老子16#^KR5c0057xtlsx016-1az10]]
Peace: to accept what must be,

![[Texts/老子/老子16#^KR5c0057xtlsx016-1az11]]
to know what endures.

![[Texts/老子/老子16#^KR5c0057xtlsx016-1az12]]
In that knowledge is wisdom.

  ![[Texts/老子/老子16#^KR5c0057xtlsx016-1az13]]
Without it,

![[Texts/老子/老子16#^KR5c0057xtlsx016-1az14]]
ruin, disorder.

![[Texts/老子/老子16#^KR5c0057xtlsx016-1az15]]
To know what endures

![[Texts/老子/老子16#^KR5c0057xtlsx016-1az16]]
is to be openhearted,

![[Texts/老子/老子16#^KR5c0057xtlsx016-1az17]]
magnanimous,

![[Texts/老子/老子16#^KR5c0057xtlsx016-1az18]]
regal, blessed,

![[Texts/老子/老子16#^KR5c0057xtlsx016-1az19]]
following the Tao,

![[Texts/老子/老子16#^KR5c0057xtlsx016-1az20]]
the way that endures forever.

![[Texts/老子/老子16#^KR5c0057xtlsx016-1az21]]
The body comes to its ending, but there is nothing to fear.
