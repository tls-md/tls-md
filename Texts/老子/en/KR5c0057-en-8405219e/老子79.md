Keeping the contract {This chapter is equally relevant to private relationships and to political treaties. Its realistic morality is based on a mystical perception of the fullness of the Way.}

   ![[Texts/老子/老子79#^KR5c0057xtlsx079-1az3]]
After a great enmity is settled

![[Texts/老子/老子79#^KR5c0057xtlsx079-1az4]]
some enmity always remains.

![[Texts/老子/老子79#^KR5c0057xtlsx079-1az5]]
How to make peace?

![[Texts/老子/老子79#^KR5c0057xtlsx079-1az6]]
Wise souls keep their part of the contract

![[Texts/老子/老子79#^KR5c0057xtlsx079-1az7]]
and don’t make demands on others.

![[Texts/老子/老子79#^KR5c0057xtlsx079-1az8]]
People whose power is real fulfill their obligations;

![[Texts/老子/老子79#^KR5c0057xtlsx079-1az9]]
people whose power is hollow insist on their claims.

![[Texts/老子/老子79#^KR5c0057xtlsx079-1az10]]
The Way of heaven plays no favorites.

![[Texts/老子/老子79#^KR5c0057xtlsx079-1az11]]
It stays with the good.
