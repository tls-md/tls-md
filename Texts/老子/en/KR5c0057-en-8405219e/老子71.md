The sick mind {What you know without knowing you know it is the right kind of knowledge. Any other kind (conviction, theory, dogmatic belief, opinion) isn’t the right kind, and if you don’t know that, you’ll lose the Way. This chapter is an example of exactly what Lao Tzu was talking about in the last one—obscure clarity, well-concealed jade.}

   ![[Texts/老子/老子71#^KR5c0057xtlsx071-1az3]]
To know without knowing is best.

![[Texts/老子/老子71#^KR5c0057xtlsx071-1az4]]
Not knowing without knowing it is sick.

![[Texts/老子/老子71#^KR5c0057xtlsx071-1az5]]
To be sick of sickness

![[Texts/老子/老子71#^KR5c0057xtlsx071-1az6]]
is the only cure.

![[Texts/老子/老子71#^KR5c0057xtlsx071-1az7]]
The wise aren’t sick.

![[Texts/老子/老子71#^KR5c0057xtlsx071-1az8]]
They’re sick of sickness,

![[Texts/老子/老子71#^KR5c0057xtlsx071-1az9]]
so they’re well.
