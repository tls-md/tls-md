Raw silk and uncut wood {This chapter and the two before it may be read as a single movement of thought.“Raw silk” and “uncut wood” are images traditionally associated with the characters su (simple, plain) and p’u (natural, honest).}

   ![[Texts/老子/老子19#^KR5c0057xtlsx019-1az3]]
Stop being holy, forget being prudent,

![[Texts/老子/老子19#^KR5c0057xtlsx019-1az4]]
it’ll be a hundred times better for everyone.

![[Texts/老子/老子19#^KR5c0057xtlsx019-1az5]]
Stop being altruistic, forget being righteous,

![[Texts/老子/老子19#^KR5c0057xtlsx019-1az6]]
people will remember what family feeling is.

![[Texts/老子/老子19#^KR5c0057xtlsx019-1az7]]
Stop planning, forget making a profit,

![[Texts/老子/老子19#^KR5c0057xtlsx019-1az8]]
there won’t be any thieves and robbers.

![[Texts/老子/老子19#^KR5c0057xtlsx019-1az9]]
But even these three rules

![[Texts/老子/老子19#^KR5c0057xtlsx019-1az10]]
needn’t be followed; what works reliably

![[Texts/老子/老子19#^KR5c0057xtlsx019-1az11]]
is to know the raw silk,

![[Texts/老子/老子19#^KR5c0057xtlsx019-1az12]]
hold the uncut wood.

![[Texts/老子/老子19#^KR5c0057xtlsx019-1az13]]
Need little,want less.
