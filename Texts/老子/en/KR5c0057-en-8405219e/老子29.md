 # ![[Texts/老子/老子29#^KR5c0057xtlsx029-1az3-h]]
Not doing {For Lao Tzu, “moderation in all things” isn’t just a bit of safe, practical advice. To lose the sense of the sacredness of the world is a mortal loss. To injure our world by excesses of greed and ingenuity is to endanger our own sacredness.}

  ![[Texts/老子/老子29#^KR5c0057xtlsx029-1az3]]
Those who think to win the world by doing something to it,

![[Texts/老子/老子29#^KR5c0057xtlsx029-1az4]]
I see them come to grief.

![[Texts/老子/老子29#^KR5c0057xtlsx029-1az5]]
For the world is a sacred object.

![[Texts/老子/老子29#^KR5c0057xtlsx029-1az6]]
Nothing is to be done to it.

![[Texts/老子/老子29#^KR5c0057xtlsx029-1az7]]
To do anything to it is to damage it.

![[Texts/老子/老子29#^KR5c0057xtlsx029-1az8]]
To seize it is to lose it.

  ![[Texts/老子/老子29#^KR5c0057xtlsx029-1az9]]
Under heaven some things lead, some follow,

![[Texts/老子/老子29#^KR5c0057xtlsx029-1az10]]
some blow hot, some cold,

![[Texts/老子/老子29#^KR5c0057xtlsx029-1az11]]
some are strong, some weak,

![[Texts/老子/老子29#^KR5c0057xtlsx029-1az12]]
some are fulfilled, some fail.

![[Texts/老子/老子29#^KR5c0057xtlsx029-1az13]]
So the wise soul keeps away from the extremes,

![[Texts/老子/老子29#^KR5c0057xtlsx029-1az14]]
excess,

![[Texts/老子/老子29#^KR5c0057xtlsx029-1az15]]
 extravagance.
