 # ![[Texts/老子/老子67#^KR5c0057xtlsx067-1az3-h]]
Three treasures {The first two verses of this chapter are a joy to me.The three final verses are closely connected in thought to the next two chapters, which may be read as a single meditation on mercy, moderation, and modesty, on the use of strength, on victory and defeat.}

   ![[Texts/老子/老子67#^KR5c0057xtlsx067-1az3]]
Everybody says my way is great

![[Texts/老子/老子67#^KR5c0057xtlsx067-1az4]]
but improbable.

![[Texts/老子/老子67#^KR5c0057xtlsx067-1az5]]
All greatness is improbable.

![[Texts/老子/老子67#^KR5c0057xtlsx067-1az6]]
What’s probable

![[Texts/老子/老子67#^KR5c0057xtlsx067-1az7]]
is tedious

![[Texts/老子/老子67#^KR5c0057xtlsx067-1az8]]
and petty.

![[Texts/老子/老子67#^KR5c0057xtlsx067-1az9]]
I have three treasures.

![[Texts/老子/老子67#^KR5c0057xtlsx067-1az10]]
I keep and treasure them.

![[Texts/老子/老子67#^KR5c0057xtlsx067-1az11]]
The first, mercy,

![[Texts/老子/老子67#^KR5c0057xtlsx067-1az12]]
the second, moderation,

![[Texts/老子/老子67#^KR5c0057xtlsx067-1az13]]
the third, modesty.

![[Texts/老子/老子67#^KR5c0057xtlsx067-1az14]]
If you’re merciful you can be brave,

![[Texts/老子/老子67#^KR5c0057xtlsx067-1az15]]
if you’re moderate you can be generous,

![[Texts/老子/老子67#^KR5c0057xtlsx067-1az16]]
and if you don’t presume to lead you can lead the high and mighty.

  ![[Texts/老子/老子67#^KR5c0057xtlsx067-1az17]]
But to be brave without compassion,

![[Texts/老子/老子67#^KR5c0057xtlsx067-1az18]]
or generous without self-restraint,

![[Texts/老子/老子67#^KR5c0057xtlsx067-1az19]]
or to take the lead,

![[Texts/老子/老子67#^KR5c0057xtlsx067-1az20]]
is fatal.

![[Texts/老子/老子67#^KR5c0057xtlsx067-1az21]]
Compassion wins the battle

![[Texts/老子/老子67#^KR5c0057xtlsx067-1az22]]
and holds the fort;

![[Texts/老子/老子67#^KR5c0057xtlsx067-1az23]]
it is the bulwark set

![[Texts/老子/老子67#^KR5c0057xtlsx067-1az24]]
around those heaven helps.
