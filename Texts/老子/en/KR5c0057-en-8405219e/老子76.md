Hardness {In an age when hardness is supposed to be the essence of strength, and even the beauty of women is reduced nearly to the bone, I welcome this reminder that tanks and tombstones are not very adequate role models, and that to be alive is to be vulnerable.}

   ![[Texts/老子/老子76#^KR5c0057xtlsx076-1az3]]
Living people are soft and tender.

![[Texts/老子/老子76#^KR5c0057xtlsx076-1az4]]
Corpses are hard and stiff.

![[Texts/老子/老子76#^KR5c0057xtlsx076-1az5]]
The ten thousand things, the living grass, the trees, are soft, pliant.

![[Texts/老子/老子76#^KR5c0057xtlsx076-1az6]]
Dead, they’re dry and brittle.

![[Texts/老子/老子76#^KR5c0057xtlsx076-1az7]]


![[Texts/老子/老子76#^KR5c0057xtlsx076-1az8]]
So hardness and stiffness go with death;

![[Texts/老子/老子76#^KR5c0057xtlsx076-1az9]]
tenderness, softness, go with life.

![[Texts/老子/老子76#^KR5c0057xtlsx076-1az10]]
And the hard sword fails,

![[Texts/老子/老子76#^KR5c0057xtlsx076-1az11]]
the stiff tree’s felled.

![[Texts/老子/老子76#^KR5c0057xtlsx076-1az12]]
The hard and great go under.

![[Texts/老子/老子76#^KR5c0057xtlsx076-1az13]]
The soft and weak stay up.
