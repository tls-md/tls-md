 # ![[Texts/老子/老子42#^KR5c0057xtlsx042-1az3-h]]
Children of the Way {Beginning with a pocket cosmology, this chapter demonstrates the “interplay of energy” of yin and yang by showing how low and high, winning and losing, destruction and self-destruction, reverse themselves, each turning into its seeming opposite.}

   ![[Texts/老子/老子42#^KR5c0057xtlsx042-1az3]]
The Way bears one.

![[Texts/老子/老子42#^KR5c0057xtlsx042-1az4]]
The one bears two.

![[Texts/老子/老子42#^KR5c0057xtlsx042-1az5]]
The two bear three.

![[Texts/老子/老子42#^KR5c0057xtlsx042-1az6]]
The three bear the ten thousand things.

![[Texts/老子/老子42#^KR5c0057xtlsx042-1az7]]
The ten thousand things carry the yin on their shoulders

![[Texts/老子/老子42#^KR5c0057xtlsx042-1az8]]
and hold in their arms the yang,

![[Texts/老子/老子42#^KR5c0057xtlsx042-1az9]]
whose interplay of energy makes harmony.

  ![[Texts/老子/老子42#^KR5c0057xtlsx042-1az10]]
People despise

![[Texts/老子/老子42#^KR5c0057xtlsx042-1az11]]
orphans, widowers, outcasts.

![[Texts/老子/老子42#^KR5c0057xtlsx042-1az12]]
Yet that’s what kings and rulers call themselves.

![[Texts/老子/老子42#^KR5c0057xtlsx042-1az13]]
Whatever you lose, you’ve won.

![[Texts/老子/老子42#^KR5c0057xtlsx042-1az14]]
Whatever you win, you’ve lost.

![[Texts/老子/老子42#^KR5c0057xtlsx042-1az15]]
What others teach,

![[Texts/老子/老子42#^KR5c0057xtlsx042-1az16]]
I say too:

![[Texts/老子/老子42#^KR5c0057xtlsx042-1az17]]
violence and aggression

![[Texts/老子/老子42#^KR5c0057xtlsx042-1az18]]
destroy themselves.

![[Texts/老子/老子42#^KR5c0057xtlsx042-1az19]]
My teaching rests on that.
