Lowdown {One of the things I love in Lao Tzu is his good cheer, as in this poem, which while giving good counsel is itself a praise and enjoyment of the spirit of yin, the water-soul that yields, follows, eludes, and leads on, dancing in the hundred valleys.}

   ![[Texts/老子/老子66#^KR5c0057xtlsx066-1az3]]
Lakes and rivers are lords of the hundred valleys.

![[Texts/老子/老子66#^KR5c0057xtlsx066-1az4]]
Why? Because they’ll go lower.

![[Texts/老子/老子66#^KR5c0057xtlsx066-1az5]]
So they’re the lords of the hundred valleys.

![[Texts/老子/老子66#^KR5c0057xtlsx066-1az6]]
Just so, a wise soul, wanting to be above other people,

![[Texts/老子/老子66#^KR5c0057xtlsx066-1az7]]
talks to them from below

![[Texts/老子/老子66#^KR5c0057xtlsx066-1az8]]
and to guide them

![[Texts/老子/老子66#^KR5c0057xtlsx066-1az9]]
follows them.

![[Texts/老子/老子66#^KR5c0057xtlsx066-1az10]]
And so the wise soul predominates

![[Texts/老子/老子66#^KR5c0057xtlsx066-1az11]]
without dominating,

![[Texts/老子/老子66#^KR5c0057xtlsx066-1az12]]
and leads

![[Texts/老子/老子66#^KR5c0057xtlsx066-1az13]]
without misleading.

![[Texts/老子/老子66#^KR5c0057xtlsx066-1az14]]
And people don’t get tired of enjoying

![[Texts/老子/老子66#^KR5c0057xtlsx066-1az15]]
and praising

![[Texts/老子/老子66#^KR5c0057xtlsx066-1az16]]
one who, not competing,

![[Texts/老子/老子66#^KR5c0057xtlsx066-1az17]]
has in all the world no competitor.
