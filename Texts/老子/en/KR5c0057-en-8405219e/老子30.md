 # ![[Texts/老子/老子30#^KR5c0057xtlsx030-1az3-h]]
Not making war {This first direct statement of Lao Tzu’s pacifism is connected in thought to the previous poem and leads directly to the next.The last verse is enigmatic: “Things flourish then perish”—How can this supremely natural sequence not be the Way? I offer my understanding of it in the note on the page with chapter 55, where nearly the same phrase occurs.}

A Taoist wouldn’t advise a ruler

to use force of arms for conquest;

![[Texts/老子/老子30#^KR5c0057xtlsx030-1az5]]
