# [[耆闍崛山]] *[[qí dū jué shān]]*  [[uuid-c43c0bab-2810-42a4-a6be-e4641d9b6632|NPpr]] [[uuid-2e7204ae-4771-435b-82ff-310068296b6d|buddhist]] [[MOUNTAINS]]
(BUDDH:) 'Vulture Peak', skr. Grdhrakūta, the sight of many sermons of the Buddha, situated in the state of Magadha (also referred to as Mt. Língqiū 靈鶖山 since the shape of the peak of the mountain resembled a stork (another explanation is that Qiū birds used to perch on its peak)
 - [[祖堂集]] 迦葉與五百弟子在**耆闍崛山**， / Kāśyapa and five hundred disciples were at Mt. Gṛdhrakūṭa,[HXWD](https://hxwd.org/textview.html?location=KR6q0002_Yan_001-1024a.21)
